Beginner paths assume **no prior experience**. They start from the fundamentals and
build up gradually, with lots of practice along the way. If you're new to a subject —
or coming back after a long break — start here.

## Who beginner paths are for

- Complete newcomers who want a guided, no-jargon start.
- Returning learners who want to rebuild confidence.
- Anyone who prefers small steps and frequent wins.

## Popular beginner paths

### First Steps in Coding

Learn how programs think. You'll cover variables, logic, loops, and write your first
small program by the end.

- **Length:** ~4 courses
- **Time:** 6–8 hours total
- **You'll be able to:** read simple code and write short scripts.

### Everyday Data

Make numbers work for you. Spreadsheets, charts, and reading data with confidence.

- **Length:** ~3 courses
- **Time:** 4–6 hours total
- **You'll be able to:** build a chart and summarize a dataset.

### Study Smarter

Learning techniques that make everything else easier — spaced repetition, active
recall, and focus habits.

- **Length:** ~2 courses
- **Time:** 2–3 hours total
- **You'll be able to:** build a study routine that sticks.

## What to expect

- Short lessons (5–10 minutes) with immediate practice.
- Frequent recaps so nothing slips away.
- A gentle difficulty curve — no big jumps.
- Encouraging feedback that explains *why*, not just *what*.

## A suggested first week

| Day | Focus                        | Time    |
| --- | ---------------------------- | ------- |
| 1   | Placement quiz + first lesson | 15 min  |
| 2   | Two lessons + practice        | 20 min  |
| 3   | Review yesterday, one lesson  | 15 min  |
| 4   | Two lessons                   | 20 min  |
| 5   | Review + mini project         | 25 min  |
| 6–7 | Rest or catch up              | —       |
